## Topics Covered

### 1. Laptop Hardware
Studied the main internal and external components of laptops, including:

- Battery (power source and charging cycles)
- Keyboard (input device and replacement considerations)
- Memory (RAM)
- Storage (SSD vs HDD and upgrade possibilities)
- Wireless and Bluetooth modules (network connectivity)
- Biometrics (fingerprint readers and authentication)
- NFC (short-range wireless communication)
- Camera / Webcam (video communication and security considerations)

---

### 2. Mobile Devices Connectivity

Learned how mobile devices connect to other devices and networks:

- USB and USB-C connections (data transfer and charging)
- Lightning connector (Apple devices)
- NFC (contactless communication)
- Bluetooth (short-range wireless communication)
- Hotspot / Tethering (sharing internet connection)

---

### 3. Mobile Device Accessories

Reviewed common accessories used with laptops and mobile devices:

- Stylus (input and drawing)
- Headsets and speakers (audio communication)
- Camera / Webcam (video conferencing)
- Docking station (expands ports and connectivity)
- Port replicator (simplified port expansion)
- Trackpad (input device)
- Drawing pad (graphics and design input)

---

### 4. Mobile Device Networks

Learned the different network technologies used by mobile devices:

- Cellular networks (mobile carrier connectivity)
- 3G, 4G LTE, and 5G technologies (speed and performance improvements)
- Wi-Fi connectivity
- Mobile hotspots
- SIM and eSIM (device identification on mobile networks)
- Bluetooth pairing
- GPS (location tracking and navigation)

---

### 5. Mobile Device Management (MDM)

Studied how organizations manage and secure mobile devices:

- MDM (Mobile Device Management) for remote control and security
- BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)
- COPE (Corporate-Owned, Personally Enabled)
- Mobile device synchronization (email, contacts, company data)
- Business applications deployment and control

---

## Lab / Practical Experience

- Understood how companies manage employee devices using MDM

## Key Concepts Learned

- Mobile devices are an important part of enterprise environments.
- MDM allows companies to secure and manage employee devices remotely.
- Modern laptops integrate wireless, biometric, and network technologies.
- Cellular and wireless technologies enable mobile productivity.
